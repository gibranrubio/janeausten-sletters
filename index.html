The economics of letter writing

Jane claimed she had "attained the true art of letter writing, which is to say on paper exactly what you would say to a person by word of mouth."

I've been talking to you as fast as I could the whole of this letter.

‚Äî Jane to Cassandra

But there's genius in the cramped handwriting. Postage was paid by the recipient and charged by sheets and distance. Neither sister had money to spare, so Jane packed entire conversations onto single pages.
